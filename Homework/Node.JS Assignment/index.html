The battle of Valley Forge during the American Revolution was vital to showcasing America's strength in the war. Over 2,000 men died from lack of shelter, disease, starvation. However, the British were camped nearby in comfort within buildings and lodges. Washington's army recieved training from Baron von Steuben in the meantime. Through intensive military tactics training the american army became a cohesive and disciplined unit. By the time spring arrived, Washington led his men against the British and overcame them in many battles. This story represents the american way to overcome hardships and perserving with endurance.
